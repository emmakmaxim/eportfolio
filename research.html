User research blogs
(Government Digital Service)

I wrote a number of blogs describing my user research approach while working at Government Digital Service (GDS) in the UK. This writing allowed the public to see the user research processes we used in government. These blogs also served as examples that professionals within and outside of government could use to inform their own approach to user research.

Sterling Store personas
(IBM)

During my career I have built and refined several personas using results from a variety of research techniques. These range from industry research and contextual inquiries to client interviews and usability testing. This is a slide from a deck that I created to build and evolve personas that were used in the IBM Commerce Order Management Solutions portfolio. These personas allowed my team to have a common, user-centred language for design and development discussions.

Book Room optimisation participatory design project
(Frontier College)

A group of masters students, including myself, designed and implemented a participatory design project to help volunteers in Frontier College's Book Room to fulfil orders more efficiently. We used a combination of interviews, content markup activities, an organisational kit game, diary keeping, and prototype testing to find the best solution for the group. This solution was implemented for several years after the project ended.
